Mental Health

Your mental health is just as important as your physical health.




About Mental Health

---------------

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It encompasses the ability to manage stress, maintain fulfilling relationships, work productively, make rational decisions, and cope with life's challenges. Good mental health is vital for overall well-being and quality of life.

Difference Between Mental Health and Mental Illness:

Mental health and mental illness are not the same. Mental health represents a state of emotional well-being, while mental illness refers to specific conditions that affect mental health negatively. Mental health is a continuum, with people varying in their mental well-being at different times. Mental illnesses include conditions like depression, anxiety disorders, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia. Having a mental illness does not mean one lacks mental health.

Significance of Maintaining Good Mental Health:

---------------

Maintaining good mental health is essential for several reasons:
- It enhances our ability to cope with life's challenges and stress.
- It promotes positive relationships and social interactions.
- Good mental health is linked to physical health, as mental and physical health are interconnected.
- It improves decision-making and productivity.
- It contributes to an overall sense of well-being and happiness.

Factors Affecting Mental Health:

A. Genetics: Genetics can play a role in predisposing individuals to certain mental health conditions, although they are not the sole determinants. Family history of mental illness may increase the risk. B. Environment: Environmental factors like early life experiences, exposure to trauma, or a supportive and nurturing environment can impact mental health.
C. Lifestyle: Lifestyle choices, such as diet, exercise, and substance use, influence mental health. A healthy lifestyle can promote better mental well-being.

About Mental Health

Stress and Trauma can significantly affect mental health:

- Stress: Everyday stress is a natural part of life. However, chronic stress can lead to mental health issues, including anxiety and depression.

- Trauma: Experiencing traumatic events, such as accidents, abuse, or violence, can result in conditions like post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Seeking professional help is crucial for those who have experienced trauma.

Signs of Symptoms of Poor Mental Health


Sleep or Appetite Changes

Dramatic sleep and appetite changes or decline in personal care.


Severe Mood Swings

You may feel sad or hopeless and uninterested in doing things you typically enjoy. In this phase, it can lead to symptoms like feeling guilty or worthless, leading to weight changes, being tired and unenergetic.


Withdrawn

Turning down invitations and avoiding social interactions and situations, from parties and events to casual gatherings with friends and family.


Drop in Functioning

An unusual drop in functioning, at school, work or social activities, such as quitting sports, failing in school or difficulty performing familiar tasks.


Problems Thinking

Problems with concentration, memory or logical thought and speech that are hard to explain.

Suicidal Thoughts

Suicidal ideation involves actively planning suicide, where as suicidal thoughts do not necessarily indicate a person has the intention to end their life.

Signs and Symptoms of Good Mental Health


Sleep or Appetite Changes

Dramatic sleep and appetite changes or decline in personal care.


Build Relationships

Investing time and energy in your relationships with family and friends is important for your mental health. It will helps you recover from life's setbacks and hardships.


Feeling Physically Healthy

A state of well-being when all internal and external body parts, organs, tissues and cells can function properly as they are supposed to function.


Confidence

Whether you're looking to cope with a specific mental health problem, handle your emotions better, or simply to feel more positive and energetic.


Getting Enough Sleep

Good sleep improves your brain performance, mood, and health. Not getting enough quality sleep regularly raises the risk of many diseases and disorders.

Set Goals and Priorities

Decide what must get done now and what can wait. Mindful of what you have accomplished at the end of the day.
